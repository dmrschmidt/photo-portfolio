§ II — The Atelier

Dennis
Schmidt.

Dennis Schmidt (b. 1987) is a German photographer working between Berlin and the northern coast. His practice moves quietly at the threshold of the observed and the remembered — images that refuse to fully resolve, held taut between silence, weather, and interior atmosphere.

Trained first as an architect, Schmidt approaches the photograph as a built thing: a structure of absences, held together by light. He works slowly. A single edition may take two winters to close. Each print is prepared by hand in the atelier on Hahnemühle Photo Rag Baryta, numbered in graphite, signed, and sealed with the house mark.

Works have entered private collections in Zürich, Copenhagen, New York and Kyoto, as well as two institutional holdings that Schmidt has requested remain unnamed. Éditions I–X constitutes the current catalogue. Once an impression is released, it is not reprinted, reissued, or made available in any further format.
